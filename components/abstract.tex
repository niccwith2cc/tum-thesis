\begin{center}
{\Large \textbf{Abstract}}
\end{center}
\vspace{1cm}

This thesis tackles algorithmic improvements implemented on a sampling based motion planner used to autonomously drive a vehicle.
The improvements involve vectorizing sampling many paths at once alongside vectorizing the loss calculation used to tweak the parameters of the differentiable motion planner.
Finally, an investigation is done between a temporal planner along side a spatial planner to contrast and compare their characteristics.
